Remdesivir

Abstract


Remdesivir has broad-spectrum antiviral action against many single-stranded RNA viruses including pathogenic SAR-CoV and the Middle East respiratory syndrome coronavirus (MERS-CoV). In this study, we proposed that remdesivir strongly binds to membrane protein (Mprotein), RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RDRP), and main protease (Mprotease) of SARS- CO V-2. The structure dynamics study suggested binding of remdesivir leads to unfolding of RDRP. Binding of remdesivir to Mprotease, Mprotein, and RDRP reduces the average motions in protein due to its strong binding. Remdesivir is a metabolically active from (GS-441524) that works by inhibiting viral RNA-dependent RNA polymerase (RDRP) while evading proofreading by viral exoribonuclease, which leads to premature termination of viral RNA transcription and hence inhibits the spread of the virus as well as the production of viral RNA (Wang M. et al., 2020). It showed possible binding and inhibitory mechanisms of remdesivir on the C-terminal domain of SARS-CoV-2 nucleocapsid protein (CTD), envelope protein (Eprotein), main protease (Mprotease), membranes protein (Mprotease), membrane phosphoprotein (NTD), RDRP, and spike protein (Sprotein) using several computational approaches. It shows strong inhibitory effects on Mprotein, RDRP, and spike protein (Sprotein, Figure 1G) using several computational approaches. It shows strong inhibitory effects on Mprotein, RDRP (Figure 1D), and Mprotease of SARS-CoV-2.
